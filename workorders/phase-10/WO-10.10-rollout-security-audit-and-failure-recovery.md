## Success Metrics
- One command gates release with all required marketplace/security checks.
- Failure drills show safe degraded behavior without data corruption.
- Kill-switches can pause billing or publication independently.
- Rollback procedure restores previous stable behavior with no ledger mismatch.

## Potential Failure Modes And Preventions
- Failure mode: rollout succeeds while fraud controls are disabled.
  Prevention: fraud-control readiness is a hard gate, not informational.
- Failure mode: rollback drops pending settlement records.
  Prevention: append-only settlement journal and replayable compensating entries.
- Failure mode: kill-switch blocks health endpoints and obscures diagnostics.
  Prevention: keep health/read-only diagnostics available under all switch states.
- Failure mode: partial gate execution hides failing subsystem.
  Prevention: consolidated artifact includes per-stage required/optional status and raw error payloads.
